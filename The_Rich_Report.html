U.S. Economics

8
Labor Market
Private payrolls fell 86,000 in February, missing expectations of a 60,000 gain, while January's strong 172,000 gain was revised down.3
Economic Outlook
The economy expanded 2.2% in 2025 and appears to be starting 2026 at a similar tepid pace. While concerns persist around elevated government spending since COVID, inflation remaining above the Fed's 2% target, and overvalued stocks, positive factors include continued technological innovation and spreading AI adoption potentially boosting productivity.3
Corporate Earnings
S&P 500 earnings growth reached 14.2% year-over-year in Q4 2025, marking the fifth consecutive quarter of double-digit growth. All eleven sectors reported positive earnings growth, led by Information Technology (33.4%), Industrials (26.7%), and Communication Services (13.1%).3
Services Sector
The ISM Non-Manufacturing index jumped to 56.1 in February, beating expectations and reaching its highest level since July 2022. Fourteen of eighteen service industries reported growth, with business activity climbing to 59.9 and new orders surging to 58.6.3
Consumer Debt
U.S. household debt reached $18.78 trillion in Q4 2025, up $740 billion for the year. Mortgage balances comprise 70% of total debt at $13.17 trillion. Aggregate delinquency rates rose to 4.8%, the highest since late 2019, driven primarily by student loan delinquencies climbing to 16.4%.3
Retail Sales
Retail sales fell 0.2% in January, slightly better than the expected 0.3% decline, as severe winter weather disrupted consumer activity. Health and personal care stores saw the largest drop (3.0%) since COVID lockdowns.3
Fixed Income
The 10-year Treasury yield stood at 3.94% at end-February, down 27 basis points from a year earlier. Gold prices surged 22.2% YTD to $5,171.74 per troy ounce despite pulling back from January's record high of $5,417.21.3
Energy Markets
Crude oil prices surged 16.72% YTD through February, driven by escalating U.S.-Iran conflict and threats to Middle Eastern shipping routes. Strait of Hormuz traffic plummeted from 104 ships on Feb 27 to just three on March 3.3

Summary

In regard to the United States Economy, private payrolls unexpectedly fell in February amid temporary factors like severe weather and labor strikes, though economists suggest recent months together show modest underlying gains. Corporate earnings remained robust with the S&P 500 posting its fifth consecutive quarter of double-digit growth at fourteen-point-two percent. However, retail sales declined and household debt reached record levels with rising delinquency rates as pandemic-era forbearance programs ended.

In the world of digital assets, Coinbase launched stock and ETF trading capabilities marking its strategic expansion into traditional securities, while Kraken achieved direct Federal Reserve payment infrastructure access enabling seamless fiat transfers. Bitcoin demonstrated remarkable resilience during the Iran conflict, outpacing traditional safe havens and equities despite persistent bearish sentiment. Senate negotiations on comprehensive crypto legislation remain stalled over disagreements regarding regulatory jurisdiction between SEC and CFTC.

Recently around the globe, the Middle East conflict intensified with U.S. submarine operations sinking Iranian vessels and crude oil prices surging as Strait of Hormuz shipping traffic plummeted dramatically.
