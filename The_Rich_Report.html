SECTION 01

U.S. Economics News

Manufacturing Expansion
The ISM Manufacturing Index hit 52.4 in February, marking consecutive months above 50 for the first time since early 2025. New orders reached 55.8, the highest reading in nearly four years outside of January, while order backlogs accelerated to 56.6 after 39 consecutive months of contraction, signaling manufacturers can finally reduce reliance on depleting inventories.3
Inflation Pressures
Producer prices surged 0.5% in January, driven by a 14.4% spike in machinery and equipment wholesaling margins, pushing the prices paid index to 70.5. Despite the monthly jump concentrated in services (up 0.8%), year-over-year producer price growth moderated to 2.9%, suggesting temporary volatility rather than sustained inflationary momentum.3
Tariff Whiplash
The Supreme Court struck down Trump’s IEEPA tariffs on February 20, invalidating roughly $170 billion in collections, before a new 10% Section 122 tariff took effect. China’s effective tariff rate dropped from 31% to 21%, while the 2025 average of 7.7% remains the highest since 1947 — even with legal setbacks, structural tariff rates will stay elevated at roughly 5.6% in 2026.3
Equity Rotation
Technology stocks shed 3.4% year-to-date through February 20 despite generating 53.6% of revenue overseas, while defensive sectors led gains — Energy (+22.7%), Materials (+16.3%), and Industrials (+14.3%) — all with significant international exposure, marking a broad rotation away from last year’s tech dominance.3
Earnings Momentum
S&P 500 earnings growth estimates rose to 13.3% for 2025 and 13.7% for 2026, up from 11.2% and 12.8% in October. Information Technology leads with projected 32.7% earnings growth in 2026, while all eleven sectors now show positive earnings estimates after Energy and Consumer Staples previously faced declines.3
Geopolitical Tensions
Oil prices jumped over 8% overnight following U.S. and Israeli strikes on Iran that killed the Ayatollah and successor, triggering the largest U.S. military buildup in the Middle East since the 2003 Iraq invasion. Treasury yields fell below 4% for the first time since November as investors fled to safety, though markets remain overvalued compared to 2003’s post-dot-com undervaluation.3

SECTION 02

Digital Assets News

Iran Crypto Shadow Economy
Iran has constructed a sophisticated $7.8 billion cryptocurrency infrastructure to circumvent crippling U.S. economic sanctions, leveraging digital assets to facilitate international trade, access global financial markets, and maintain economic lifelines. This parallel financial system enables the regime to move value across borders while evading traditional banking restrictions, representing one of the most significant state-level adoptions of cryptocurrency for sanctions evasion.5
Clarity Act
JPMorgan identifies potential passage of the Clarity Act by mid-2026 as a significant catalyst for the cryptocurrency sector, as this legislation would establish comprehensive regulatory frameworks for digital assets, reducing legal uncertainty that has long hindered institutional adoption and potentially unlocking substantial capital inflows from traditional financial institutions that have remained cautious amid ambiguous regulatory guidance.5
Banking Lobby Resistance
Traditional banks view interest-bearing stablecoins as an existential threat to their deposit-based business model, which relies on paying minimal interest while lending at higher rates. The potential for consumers to shift funds into stablecoins offering competitive yields could force banks to raise deposit rates significantly, compressing profit margins and fundamentally disrupting the fractional reserve banking system that has underpinned their operations for decades.5
Hashrate Rebound
The Bitcoin network’s computational power recovered to 913 exahashes per second following a significant 20% decline from its October peak of approximately 1,141 EH/s. This drawdown, likely driven by seasonal factors, miner capitulation, or equipment upgrades, represented a notable but temporary disruption before the network’s hash rate stabilized and began its recovery trajectory.5
USDC Growth
Circle’s USD Coin (USDC) circulation reached $75 billion in early 2025, marking a 72% year-over-year increase driven by institutional adoption, expanded DeFi integration, and growing demand for stablecoin-based cross-border payments. This surge positioned USDC as the second-largest stablecoin by market capitalization, reflecting broader confidence in regulated, dollar-backed digital assets amid increased regulatory clarity.5

SECTION 03

Geopolitics News

Canada
Signed a $1.91 billion uranium supply agreement with India through Cameco covering 2027-2035, part of broader energy partnership aimed at deepening ties and reducing economic reliance on the United States. The countries agreed to conclude a Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement this year, targeting doubled bilateral trade to $70 billion by 2030.1
Germany
Approved reduced 2 billion euro framework deal for strike drone purchases, down from 4.3 billion euros previously envisioned, as lawmakers seek tighter control over future budgets. Parliament’s budget committee approved first 540 million euro order for drones from German startups Helsing and Stark Defence, limiting total contract value per company to one billion euros.1
Iran
Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei killed in US-Israeli attack on leadership compound in Tehran, with CIA having tracked him for months before learning of Saturday meeting of top officials. The 86-year-old’s death brings new urgency to succession question, with Hassan Khomeini, 53-year-old grandson of Islamic Republic founder, likely to figure prominently in clerics’ deliberations on replacement.1
Japan
China imposed tougher export controls on 20 Japanese entities including Mitsubishi Heavy Industries’ shipbuilding and aerospace affiliates, marking first time Japanese firms added to control list since January 2025 debut. Beijing also placed 20 additional entities including Subaru and Hino Motors on monitor list requiring stricter licensing checks when applying for dual-use goods licenses.1
Qatar
Halted liquefied natural gas production after intercepting two Iranian drones targeting energy facility in Ras Laffan Industrial City, major LNG hub for one of world’s top exporters. Attack marks dangerous inflection point bringing Mideast conflict to energy industry, with European natural-gas prices surging 40% as Qatar is big customer for continent.1
United States
OpenAI CEO Sam Altman told staff company working on Pentagon deal to deploy AI models in classified environments while maintaining safety guardrails, hoping to help resolve standoff between Anthropic and Defense Department. Altman said in memo company would ask for contract covering any use except unlawful purposes or those unsuited to cloud deployments like domestic surveillance and autonomous offensive weapons.1
United States
Section 122 of Trade Act of 1974 allows presidents to impose tariffs up to 15% for up to 150 days to address “fundamental international payments problems” without waiting for federal agency investigation. Trump invoked never-before-used statute after Supreme Court invalidated tariffs using emergency powers law, with congressional approval required to keep duties beyond 150 days.1

The Good News

Sugar Prices
Raw cane sugar futures dropped to 14 cents per pound, the lowest since October 2020 and less than half their late 2023 peak. GLP-1 weight-loss medications like Wegovy and Ozempic are driving sharper-than-forecast consumption declines in wealthy economies by reducing cravings for sweet flavors, catching the sugar industry off guard.2
AI Drug Discovery
Isomorphic Labs, Google DeepMind’s pharmaceutical spin-off, unveiled IsoDDE, an AI model surpassing AlphaFold3’s capabilities. The system achieves precise predictions of protein-drug interactions and antibody structures, impressing field scientists with its potential to accelerate pharmaceutical development beyond previous computational biology breakthroughs.2
Spina Bifida Treatment
Six pregnant women received experimental in-utero therapy at 24-25 weeks gestation, with placental stem cells applied directly to fetuses’ exposed spinal cords. All newborns delivered around 34 weeks showed reversed hindbrain herniation with no infections, fluid leakage, or tumor growth, offering hope for treating severe myelomeningocele affecting movement and continence.2
