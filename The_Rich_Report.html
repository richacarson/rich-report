U.S. Economics

6
Monetary Policy Stance
Fed likely to maintain current rates at March 18th meeting rather than cut, given 2.4% headline inflation and 2.5% core inflation persisting above 2% target. Oil supply shock represents relative price shift not requiring Fed intervention; easing policy to cushion consumer impact would risk creating broader inflation rather than addressing temporary commodity disruption.3
GDP Growth Weakens
Fourth quarter 2025 real GDP revised down to 0.7% annualized rate from initial 1.4%, driven by downward revisions to exports, consumption, business investment, and government spending. Core GDP (consumer spending, business investment, residential construction) grew 1.9% while government purchases shrank 5.8% annualized, largest drag since Q3 2020, contributing to slower growth paired with higher 3.8% GDP price inflation.3
Housing Market Stagnates
January housing starts rose 7.2% to 1.487 million annual rate but gain entirely from volatile multi-unit category jumping 29.9%, while single-family starts declined 2.8% and remain 6.5% below year-ago levels. Building permits fell 5.4% with single-family permits down 11.6% year-over-year; completions outpaced starts for tenth time in twelve months as builders focus on finishing projects amid elevated home prices and affordability challenges.3
Strategic Petroleum Reserve
US Strategic Petroleum Reserve held 415.4 million barrels as of March 6th, up 19.8% from July 2023 low but still 34.9% below 638.1 million when Biden took office. Reserve equates to 125 days of crude net imports with maximum 4.4 million barrel daily drawdown capacity taking 13 days to reach market after authorization; not long-term solution though could temporarily ease price pressures.3
Strait of Hormuz Disruption
Approximately 14 million of 77 million daily global crude barrels transit Strait of Hormuz, with 89% destined for Asian markets led by China (37.7%), India (14.7%), South Korea (12%), Japan (10.9%). US reduced reliance significantly with Strait flows representing only 2.5% of total volume and 6% of US crude imports in Q1 2025, limiting direct domestic supply impact compared to Asian economies.3
Energy Sector Dominance
All five S&P 500 Energy subsectors ranked among top 15 performers year-to-date through March 6th, with sector returning 26.47% led by Commodity Chemicals (49.14%), Oil & Gas Refining (35%), and Construction & Engineering (28%). Energy benefited from Iran war supply disruptions, winter storms, and investor diversification demand; WTI crude spiked 35.63% in week following February 27th war inception, supporting sector outperformance amid broader market weakness.3

Digital Assets

5
First Positive Month
March 2025 represents a potential turning point for cryptocurrency exchange-traded funds, marking the first month of net positive inflows since October 2024. This reversal follows five consecutive months of investor withdrawals and signals renewed institutional confidence in digital asset exposure through regulated investment vehicles, potentially driven by improving market sentiment and clearer regulatory frameworks.5
Iran War Impact
Bitcoin demonstrated its characteristic volatility during geopolitical tensions, initially dropping 8.5% as conflict with Iran commenced due to risk-off sentiment among investors. However, the cryptocurrency has since staged a notable recovery, climbing 11% from its recent lows as markets digested the situation and some investors sought alternative stores of value amid traditional market uncertainty.5
Market Structure Bill
The Senate is not anticipated to advance the legislation before the April timeframe, likely due to competing legislative priorities and the need to build broader bipartisan consensus. Key stakeholders continue negotiations over provisions addressing market concentration and competitive practices, with committee hearings and amendments still pending before any floor vote can be scheduled.5
103 Countries Have Established Formal Cryptocurrency Regulatory Frameworks
As of 2024, over 103 nations worldwide have implemented comprehensive legal and regulatory structures governing digital assets, ranging from licensing requirements for exchanges and custodians to consumer protection measures, anti-money laundering protocols, and taxation guidelines. This represents a significant shift from the regulatory uncertainty that characterized the crypto industry's early years, demonstrating growing institutional acceptance.5
SAB 122
The SEC issued Staff Accounting Bulletin 122 in July 2024, superseding the controversial SAB 121 from 2022. This new guidance relaxed the stringent balance sheet liability requirements that had effectively prevented traditional banks from offering crypto custody services, allowing financial institutions greater flexibility in safeguarding digital assets while maintaining appropriate accounting disclosures and risk management frameworks.5

The Good News

4
Nuclear Breakthrough
TerraPower received the first construction permit this century for a commercial fourth-generation reactor, converting a Wyoming coal plant using liquid sodium coolant instead of water. New DOE rules have dramatically reduced environmental and security requirements for experimental reactors, with President Trump's executive order targeting three operational units by July 4th to accelerate next-generation nuclear development.2
AI Drug Discovery
Machine learning is identifying treatments for previously incurable diseases in hours versus years. MIT researchers found new compounds against antibiotic-resistant MRSA and gonorrhoea, while Cambridge scientists discovered five novel Parkinson's candidates targeting Lewy body protein clumps. Harvard's AI model identified 8,000 approved drugs potentially repurposable for 17,000 different diseases, dramatically expanding treatment options.2
Electric Aviation
The FAA launched pilot projects across 26 states testing electric aircraft and eVTOLs — large electric drones carrying passengers or cargo. Eight approved projects include Manhattan air taxis, Texas regional flights, and Florida medical response, giving startups like Joby and Archer real-world testing before final certification, accelerating the transition to emissions-free flight.2
Ultra-Fast EV Charging
BYD's Blade Battery 2.0 achieves 10-70% charge in five minutes and 20-97% in twelve minutes using 1.5-megawatt chargers, matching gas station refueling speeds. The battery maintains performance in -30°C conditions and delivers over 621-mile range, eliminating the primary practical barrier to mass electric vehicle adoption.2
