U.S. Economics News:

  • Inflation Pressures: Producer prices surged 0.5% in January, driven by a 14.4% spike in machinery and equipment wholesaling margins, pushing the prices paid index to 70.5. Despite the monthly jump concentrated in services (up 0.8%), year-over-year producer price growth moderated to 2.9%, suggesting temporary volatility rather than sustained inflationary momentum.3
  • Equity Rotation: Technology stocks shed 3.4% year-to-date through February 20 despite generating 53.6% of revenue overseas, while defensive sectors led gains—Energy (+22.7%), Materials (+16.3%), and Industrials (+14.3%)—all with significant international exposure, marking a broad rotation away from last year's tech dominance.3

Digital Assets News:

  • Clarity Act: JPMorgan identifies potential passage of the Clarity Act by mid-2026 as a significant catalyst for the cryptocurrency sector, as this legislation would establish comprehensive regulatory frameworks for digital assets, reducing legal uncertainty that has long hindered institutional adoption and potentially unlocking substantial capital inflows from traditional financial institutions that have remained cautious amid ambiguous regulatory guidance.5
  • Banking Lobby Resistance: Traditional banks view interest-bearing stablecoins as an existential threat to their deposit-based business model, which relies on paying minimal interest while lending at higher rates. The potential for consumers to shift funds into stablecoins offering competitive yields could force banks to raise deposit rates significantly, compressing profit margins and fundamentally disrupting the fractional reserve banking system that has underpinned their operations for decades.5
  • Hashrate Rebound: The Bitcoin network's computational power recovered to 913 exahashes per second following a significant 20% decline from its October peak of approximately 1,141 EH/s. This drawdown, likely driven by seasonal factors, miner capitulation, or equipment upgrades, represented a notable but temporary disruption before the network's hash rate stabilized and began its recovery trajectory.5
  • USDC Growth: Circle's USD Coin (USDC) circulation reached $75 billion in early 2025, marking a 72% year-over-year increase driven by institutional adoption, expanded DeFi integration, and growing demand for stablecoin-based cross-border payments. This surge positioned USDC as the second-largest stablecoin by market capitalization, reflecting broader confidence in regulated, dollar-backed digital assets amid increased regulatory clarity.5

Geopolitics News:

  • China: Military corruption purges are leaving serious command deficiencies and likely hampering readiness of rapidly modernizing armed forces, according to International Institute for Strategic Studies annual Military Balance report. The crackdown has reduced supreme military command to just two people - Xi Jinping and newly promoted vice chairman Zhang Shengmin.1
  • China: German car exports to China plunged by roughly a third in 2025 to under 14 billion euros, down from nearly 30 billion euros in 2022, according to German Economic Institute study. The steep decline underscores rapid erosion of Germany's foothold in its most important foreign market amid intensifying price war and electric vehicle transition.1
  • China: Imposed tougher export controls on 20 Japanese entities including Mitsubishi Heavy Industries' shipbuilding and aerospace affiliates, marking first time Japanese firms added to control list since January 2025 debut. Beijing also placed 20 additional entities including Subaru and Hino Motors on monitor list requiring stricter licensing checks for dual-use goods.1
  • Malaysia: Parliament narrowly rejected constitutional amendment limiting premiership to two terms, with 146 lawmakers voting in favor falling two short of required two-thirds majority. Bill sought to limit prime minister service to 10 years as part of Anwar Ibrahim's reform agenda, but faced 44 abstentions and 32 absences amid growing discontent over reform implementation.1

GLP-1 weight-loss medications like Wegovy and Ozempic are driving sharper-than-forecast consumption declines in wealthy economies by reducing cravings for sweet flavors, catching the sugar industry off guard.2
