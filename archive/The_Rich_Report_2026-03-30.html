U.S. Economics

4
Electricity Demand
The U.S. set a record with 4.43 thousand TWh of electricity generation in 2025, and projections call for an additional 400 TWh by 2030 — equivalent to roughly 45 gigawatts of constant 24/7 power. Data centers are the primary driver of this surge, representing the steepest demand increase the country has seen in decades and creating urgent questions about generation capacity and grid infrastructure.3
Capacity Gap
Nuclear operates at a 91% capacity factor compared to 24% for solar and 34% for wind, which is why nuclear accounts for 18% of total U.S. electricity generation despite being only 8% of installed capacity. As data center demand requires reliable baseload power around the clock, this distinction between nameplate capacity and actual output becomes critical for planning the next generation of energy infrastructure.3
Rate Reversal
At the start of 2026, fed funds futures implied more than two rate cuts ahead. That expectation has completely reversed, with markets now pricing in a possible rate increase by year-end. The shift is driven by surging energy prices putting upward pressure on CPI and the geopolitical uncertainty entering the fourth week of the war with Iran, which has no clear timeline for resolution.3
K-Shaped Stress
The so-called K-shaped economy may be losing its upper leg of support. The top 20% of earners account for 63% of total U.S. spending, fueled by stock and home price gains, but with the S&P 500 back to where it was eight months ago, wealth-effect spending could stall. GDP grew just 2.0% in 2025, and the Atlanta Fed projects the same pace for Q1 2026, with more downside risk than upside given an oversized federal government that continues to crowd out private investment.3

Digital Assets

3
Clarity Act Yield Ban
The latest Clarity Act draft would bar rewards on passive stablecoin balances and ban anything “economically equivalent to interest,” threatening a key incentive that has fueled USDC adoption and drawing criticism from crypto insiders.5
Circle Plunge
Circle shares fell 20% and Coinbase dropped about 10% after the restrictive Clarity Act language emerged, snapping Circle’s 170% rally since early February.5
Mining Crisis
The average public miner spent roughly $80,000 to produce one bitcoin last quarter while BTC trades near $70,000, prompting the industry to pivot to AI with over $70 billion in contracts signed and miners liquidating bitcoin treasuries to fund the transition.5

Geopolitics

10
China
Two Chinese container ships attempted to exit the Strait of Hormuz after Iran promised safe passage for “friendly nations” but turned back, demonstrating that safe transit still cannot be guaranteed despite Tehran’s assurances.1
Egypt
The government is slowing large state projects with high fuel consumption for at least two months, cutting government vehicle fuel allocations by 30%, and mandating remote work on Sundays in April as it copes with soaring energy costs from the Iran war.1
Germany
Inflation jumped to 2.8% in March from 2.0% in February, driven by energy prices rising 7.2% year-over-year — the first energy price increase since December 2023 — as the Iran war pushes costs well above the ECB’s 2% target.1
Iran
The IRGC naval commander who led the Strait of Hormuz blockade, Alireza Tangsiri, was killed in an Israeli airstrike, though the U.S. can only confirm about a third of Iran’s missile arsenal has been destroyed, with another third likely damaged or buried in tunnels.1
Iran
Tehran has reportedly begun charging up to $2 million per ship for “safe passage” through the Hormuz Strait, with over 3,200 vessels stranded, effectively turning the critical global chokepoint into a toll booth that one Iranian lawmaker framed as covering “war costs.”1
Russia
At least 40% of Russia’s oil export capacity — roughly 2 million barrels per day — has been halted following Ukrainian drone attacks on all three major western export ports (Novorossiysk, Primorsk, Ust-Luga) and the disruption of the Druzhba pipeline, the most severe oil supply disruption in Russia’s modern history.1
United Kingdom
PM Starmer authorized the military to board and detain Russian shadow fleet tankers in British waters, joining France, Belgium, and Sweden in escalating efforts to disrupt Moscow’s sanctions-evasion oil exports as Putin benefits from surging Iran-war oil prices.1
United States
A U.S. court suspended Pentagon sanctions against Anthropic, with a San Francisco judge finding the government appeared to be punishing the AI company for criticizing the administration, likely violating free speech protections.1
United States
The Senate approved funding for TSA and most of Homeland Security after a 42-day stalemate, but excluded immigration enforcement funding, with Trump pledging to immediately pay TSA agents amid airport chaos.1
Yemen
The Houthis entered the Iran war, raising the risk of renewed attacks on the Bab al-Mandab Strait — a second critical shipping chokepoint — after Iran already effectively closed the Strait of Hormuz, which would deepen the global oil and trade crisis.1

The Good News

3
AI Drug Discovery
Insilico Medicine’s AI-identified compound rentosertib performed well in phase 2 clinical trials for idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis and is advancing to phase 3, potentially becoming the first true AI-discovered medicine to reach approval — created on an accelerated timeline and at a fraction of traditional drug discovery costs.2
EV Fast Charging
BYD is unveiling a 1,500kW drive-through charging system in Paris that can boost its Denza Z9GT from 10% to 70% battery in just five minutes and fully charge in nine minutes, potentially eliminating one of the last major obstacles to widespread EV adoption.2
GLP-1 Muscle Safety
A meta-analysis of 20 randomized controlled trials found that lean mass loss from GLP-1 drugs like semaglutide and tirzepatide is proportionally comparable to traditional lifestyle interventions alone, countering concerns that the medications cause disproportionate muscle wasting — while adding resistance training showed the best muscle-preservation profile.2

Summary

In regard to the United States Economy, electricity demand is surging to levels not seen in decades, with the U.S. setting a generation record in 2025 and projections calling for an additional 400 terawatt-hours by 2030. Meanwhile, interest rate expectations have flipped dramatically, with markets now pricing in a possible Fed rate hike by year-end after starting 2026 expecting multiple cuts. Adding to concerns, the K-shaped economy may be losing support on both ends, with the wealth effect fading as the S&P 500 stalls and GDP growth stuck.

In the world of digital assets, the Clarity Act’s latest draft sent shockwaves through the stablecoin ecosystem by banning rewards on passive balances and anything “economically equivalent to interest,” hammering Circle shares by 20% and Coinbase by 10% in a single session. Bitcoin mining economics have broken down as well, with the average public miner spending roughly $80,000 to produce one bitcoin while it trades near $70,000.

Recently around the globe, the Iran war entered its fifth week with the Houthis joining the conflict, raising the specter of a second shipping chokepoint closure at the Bab al-Mandab Strait alongside the already-shuttered Strait of Hormuz, where Tehran has begun charging vessels up to $2 million for safe passage. The killing of Iran’s IRGC naval commander by Israel underscored the campaign’s intensity, though the U.S. can only confirm a third of Iran’s missile arsenal destroyed. Ukraine’s intensified drone strikes halted 40% of Russia’s oil export capacity, prompting the UK to authorize boarding Russian shadow fleet tankers.

BYD is set to unveil a 1,500-kilowatt charging system capable of boosting an EV battery from 10% to 70% in just five minutes. In the news for the dividend portfolio, Lockheed Martin continued stacking contracts with a $478 million Navy deal and Pentagon agreements to boost missile production, while Analog Devices earned an upgrade at Aretes amid expectations of an analog chip rebound. Stryker rebounded after containing a cyberattack with minimal lasting production impact, TE Connectivity resolved its legal dispute with Credo, and Valero navigated refinery fires at two Texas facilities as the EPA expanded biofuel blending quotas to ease pump prices.
