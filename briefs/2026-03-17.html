Section 02

Geopolitics

Day 18 — Decapitation Accelerates
Israel claims it killed Ali Larijani—secretary of Iran’s Supreme National Security Council, former nuclear negotiator, former parliament speaker, and arguably the regime’s de facto wartime leader—in an overnight strike on a Tehran safe house. The IDF also claims it killed Basij paramilitary commander Gholamreza Soleimani. Iran has not confirmed either. If true, Larijani is the highest-profile assassination since the elder Khamenei on Day 1. The decapitation strategy is systematic: the US and Israel are dismantling Iran’s command structure layer by layer. The question is whether this hastens a ceasefire or hardens resistance.
Khamenei Rejects Peace
Before the Larijani strike, Mojtaba Khamenei rejected all ceasefire proposals conveyed by two intermediary countries. A senior Iranian official described his stance as “very tough and serious”—no peace until the US and Israel “accept defeat and pay compensation.” Iran continues missile salvoes at Israel and Gulf states. Debris from intercepts killed one in Abu Dhabi, injured two in Kuwait. Dubai International Airport suspended flights again after a nearby drone strike. The Fujairah oil zone took another hit.
No Coalition, No Diplomacy
EU foreign ministers declined to expand naval operations in the region. Australia and Japan publicly refused to send ships. The UK said it would not be “drawn into the wider war.” Trump claims unnamed countries are “on the way” but no one has committed publicly. Gulf oil exports are down 61% versus February. Iraq is now in separate talks with Iran to safeguard its own tanker traffic—countries are cutting bilateral deals because there is no multilateral solution.
