Oil Surges as SAMREF Hit Crosses a Red Line
Brent surged +5.59% to ~$112. WTI +2.38% to ~$101+. Nat gas +3.85%. This is the first confirmed attack on Saudi refining infrastructure since the 2019 Abqaiq/Khurais strike. It crosses a line: Iran is now targeting non-Gulf, downstream assets in allied countries. Energy was the only sector that held ground (XLE –0.14%), while staples (–2.43%), materials (–2.10%), and discretionary (–2.31%) were crushed. VLO +1.06%, CVX +0.32%, CNX –0.48%—refiners benefit from the crack spread widening.

The only place to hide was defense (LMT +0.94%) and shipping (MATX +0.60%).

Geopolitics

Day 20 — Iran Hits Saudi Refining, Israel Hits Gas Fields
The war entered a new phase overnight. Iran struck the SAMREF refinery in Yanbu, Saudi Arabia—a Red Sea facility 1,000km from the Gulf, jointly owned by Aramco and Exxon. This is Iran attacking Western-owned energy infrastructure in a third country. Simultaneously, Trump confirmed Israel struck an Iranian gas field—then said it was done “without US and Qatari involvement” and warned Israel against further attacks on Qatar-adjacent assets. Trump is publicly distancing from an Israeli escalation he didn’t authorize. The Pentagon is requesting $200B+ in supplemental war funding. US military reinforcements are being weighed. This is no longer a proxy conflict—it’s a direct great-power energy war.
Ukraine Talks Suspended, Diplomacy Freezes
Russia’s Izvestia reports Ukraine peace negotiations are “paused amid the Iran war”—the second geopolitical process frozen by the Gulf conflict. EU leaders convened an emergency session hunting “quick fixes” to the energy price spike but produced nothing concrete. Trump will press Japan’s PM for military support today. Iran executed three citizens arrested during January protests—the regime is hardening domestically even as its infrastructure is degraded. There is no diplomatic off-ramp visible from any angle.
The $200B Pentagon Budget Request
The Washington Post reports the Pentagon is seeking more than $200 billion in supplemental budget for the Iran war—the largest single-conflict request since the early Iraq/Afghanistan era. This is structurally bullish for defense primes. LMT +0.94% yesterday was the only major IOWN holding to close green outside energy. GD –0.82% lagged but remains positioned for submarine and munitions flow. The Shahed drone war is creating what SeekingAlpha calls a “missile defense supercycle” for Lockheed. The physical world keeps demanding real spending on real hardware.
