Brent’s 3.3% Friday rally was driven by fresh supply disruptions. Iraq declared force majeure at all oilfields operated by foreign companies. Drones struck two Kuwait refineries. WTI settled at $98.32, up 2.3%. The IEA’s record 400-million-barrel reserve release and the US SPR tap continue to fail to contain prices—physical market dynamics are overwhelming paper interventions. CVX +0.14% and OKE +0.93% held green while VLO slipped –0.91% with the broader selloff. Fed Rate Hike Talk Emerges — A Regime Change
The March 19 “hawkish hold” at 3.50–3.75% is reverberating through rate markets. The probability of zero cuts this year has risen to 48%. Bank of America reports clients are asking about rate hikes. Powell characterized inflation pressures as “murky” and declined to rule out further tightening if energy costs bleed into core services. The Fed upgraded its median core PCE forecast to 2.7%. Rate-sensitive sectors bore Friday’s brunt: utilities –4.06%, real estate –3.17%, NEE –3.15%, BKH –4.34%. The shift from “when do they cut” to “could they hike” is a fundamental repricing of the policy backdrop.

Section 02

Geopolitics

Day 23 — Trump’s 48-Hour Ultimatum Resets the War’s Trajectory
Saturday night, Trump posted a 48-hour deadline on Truth Social: reopen the Strait of Hormuz or the US strikes Iran’s power plants. Bloomberg and Al Jazeera both characterize this as the most consequential US ultimatum since the conflict began. The deadline expires Monday at 7:44 PM ET. This is a complete reversal from “winding down” rhetoric just 24 hours earlier—a whiplash that underscores the unpredictability of the policy framework. Iran boasts approximately 400 power plants, including the Bushehr nuclear facility on its southern coast. Targeting civilian energy infrastructure would represent a qualitative expansion of the conflict’s scope.
Iran Strikes Near Israel’s Nuclear Facility — Retaliation Cycle Deepens
Hours before Trump’s ultimatum, Iranian missiles struck the southern Israeli towns of Dimona and Arad, wounding over 100 people including children. Dimona is located approximately 13 kilometers from Israel’s main nuclear research center. Iran framed the strikes as direct retaliation for Israel’s earlier hit on Natanz. Israeli Defense Minister Katz stated attacks on Iran would “increase significantly” this week. Iran’s Khatam al-Anbiya military command warned that if Iranian energy infrastructure is targeted, all US-linked energy, IT, and desalination infrastructure in the region would face retaliation. The escalation ladder has no visible off-ramp.
Diplomatic Hardening Accelerates
Saudi Arabia expelled Iran’s military attaché and four embassy staff within 24 hours—a significant break from Riyadh’s earlier posture of cautious neutrality. Twenty-two nations issued a joint statement condemning Iran’s Hormuz blockade and attacks on commercial vessels, warning that “the effects of Iran’s actions will be felt by people in all parts of the world.” The UAE and Australia joined the coalition for safe navigation. UN Secretary-General Guterres offered to help mediate Hormuz reopening. But with Trump’s Monday deadline looming, the military track appears to be outpacing diplomatic efforts. For the portfolio, the 48-hour window creates a binary setup: if the US strikes power plants, Iran has explicitly promised regional infrastructure retaliation, which could widen the energy supply shock further. Defense holdings GD and LMT remain positioned for sustained conflict spending.
