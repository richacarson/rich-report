Markets

Software Collapses While Semis Recover — The Clearest Sector Signal of the Year
Tuesday’s session produced the sharpest intra-tech divergence of 2026. The S&P 500 fell 0.37% to 6,556, the Dow shed 0.18% to 46,124, and the Nasdaq dropped 0.84%. Index-level moves buried what was actually a dramatic rotation: energy gained 2.03% and materials 1.89%, while communication services fell 1.40% and tech slid 0.58%—but within tech, the software subset experienced a near-collapse. The IGV iShares software ETF fell more than 3% Tuesday and is now down 23% in 2026. Salesforce dropped 6.23%, ServiceNow fell more than 4%, Oracle slid nearly 5%, and Microsoft declined 2.68%. The catalyst: reports of new Amazon AI tools encroaching on the enterprise software stack amplified long-running fears that AI disruption is compressing software moats faster than current multiples reflect. IOWN holds zero pure software exposure. We own the infrastructure powering AI, not the application layer being displaced by it.
Semis Diverge Hard From Software
While software imploded, IOWN’s semiconductor sleeve recovered sharply: ADI +3.09% ($321.83), KEYS +3.33% ($298.58), LRCX +2.37% ($238.84), TSM +1.42% ($343.25), AMD +1.33% ($205.37). The catalyst was Broadcom’s disclosure that Taiwan Semiconductor capacity is essentially maxed out—a structurally bullish signal for equipment demand (LRCX), advanced node pricing (TSM), and the broader physical AI infrastructure buildout. Capacity constraints at TSMC historically precede significant capital equipment orders. LRCX, which builds the deposition equipment that enables each new chip generation, is the direct beneficiary. The physical world matters thesis is showing up in the data: when software names melt down on AI disruption fears, the companies building the foundational silicon layer are bid.
Treasury Auction Weakness — 10-Year at 4.42%
The 10-year Treasury yield rose 8 basis points to 4.42% on Tuesday following what BMO described as a “weak” auction—below-average non-dealer bidding, suggesting foreign appetite for US duration is thinning. The 2-year yield jumped 11 basis points to 3.944%, the largest single-session move in weeks. TLT fell 0.43% and has not caught a sustained bid since the war began. March flash manufacturing PMI surprised to the upside at 52.4 (expected 51.3), defying war-era pessimism on the factory floor. But the PMI narrative is splitting: manufacturing is holding; travel, tourism, and service-sector subcomponents are showing clear demand damage from elevated energy costs and financial market uncertainty. Hard data is holding. Soft data is cracking. This divergence rarely persists.

Section 02

Geopolitics

Day 25 — The 15-Point Plan Is in Tehran’s Hands. So Are IRGC’s Counter-Demands.
The United States delivered a formal 15-point ceasefire framework to Iran via Pakistani intermediaries on Wednesday morning. An Egyptian official involved in mediation described it as a “comprehensive deal” covering Hormuz reopening, missile program restrictions, and curbs on Iran’s support for proxy forces. Iran received the document. Almost simultaneously, the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps circulated its own counter-demands: closure of all US military bases in the Gulf, reparations for damage sustained in the war, the right to collect fees from all Hormuz transit, an end to Israeli strikes on Hezbollah, and full sanctions relief. The gap between the two documents defines the width of the conflict itself. Trump claimed Iran had given the US “a very big present worth a tremendous amount of money” tied to energy flows through Hormuz—likely referencing the Thai tanker transit and Iran’s selective-passage arrangement. Iran told Indian media they are “absolutely” charging fees for Hormuz passage, effectively monetizing the blockade.
Kuwait Airport Struck — US Escalates Military Deployment
Iranian forces struck Kuwait International Airport on Wednesday, sparking a large fire visible across the city. US Central Command announced deployment of 1,000 troops from the 82nd Airborne Division, with an additional 5,000 Marines and thousands of sailors heading to the region. CENTCOM has now struck more than 9,000 Iranian targets and flown over 9,000 combat flights since February 28. Netanyahu confirmed Israel will continue striking Iran and Lebanon until the war’s objectives are realized. The diplomatic track and the military track are running simultaneously in opposite directions: more troops deploying while more mediators convene. Pakistan hosted engagement between its intelligence chief and US envoys Witkoff and Kushner. A Vance-attended meeting in Pakistan is being discussed but not confirmed. China’s Wang Yi told Araghchi to “seize every window for peace.” Iran’s Foreign Ministry called US diplomacy untrustworthy and said there are no direct negotiations in place.
