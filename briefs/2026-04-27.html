Powell’s Probe Dropped Friday — Warsh Confirmation Path Now Open
The DOJ closed its criminal probe of Fed Chair Powell on Friday afternoon, per CNBC and Al Jazeera coverage citing US Attorney for DC Jeannine Pirro, removing the procedural barrier that had blocked Kevin Warsh’s confirmation. Senator Tillis had conditioned his vote on closure of the investigation. With Powell’s term as Chair expiring May 15, the Senate now has a clear runway to confirm Warsh ahead of that date. The transition matters for IOWN’s rate-sensitive positioning: Warsh has historically been associated with a more hawkish posture on inflation expectations than the current FOMC consensus. The April 28–29 meeting will be Powell’s penultimate as Chair, and the press conference is likely to be parsed for any signal on the succession framework. JPMorgan Research and Morgan Stanley both sit at hold-through-2026 on the rate path, with the first cut now priced toward late 2026 per recent Reuters polling.

Geopolitics

Iran Talks Stalled Over the Weekend — Trump Halted Discussions, Pezeshkian Rejected “Imposed Negotiations”
The 3–5 day engagement window from the April 21 ceasefire extension closed without resolution. Per Washington Post, Al Jazeera, and CBS News coverage, Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi briefly visited Islamabad on Sunday as Pakistani mediators worked to revive talks, but Trump cancelled the planned US envoy trip and stated the sides could speak by phone instead. Iranian President Pezeshkian publicly rejected the framework, characterizing the US posture as coercive and conditional on the lifting of the naval blockade. The structural impasse remains unchanged: Iran insists the US naval blockade on Iranian ports be lifted before substantive nuclear talks resume, while Trump has tied the pause on US strikes to the full reopening of Hormuz transit. A senior Iranian official stated over the weekend that Hormuz transit will not return to its prior state under any circumstances. The conflict has now entered its ninth week, with the IEA continuing to characterize the disruption as the largest energy supply shock on record.
Oil Firmed Overnight on the Hormuz Standoff — Brent Back Toward $107
Brent crude rose toward $107 per barrel overnight, recouping a portion of last week’s drift lower as the diplomatic track stalled. WTI traded near $96 per barrel, per CNBC and Bloomberg coverage. The move reverses the late-week compression of the war premium and reinforces the higher-for-longer commodity inflation thesis underlying the Fed’s hold posture. The IOWN energy sleeve carries the structural exposure: VLO closed Friday at $235.85 (+0.86%), CVX at $185.21 (–1.27%), CTRA at $33.53 (–0.36%), and OKE at $87.50 (+0.33%). Refining margins remain elevated relative to pre-conflict baselines per Morgan Stanley research, supporting the VLO and OKE thesis. The dispersion between energy inflation and disinflationary tech earnings will be the principal cross-current the committee should track this week, particularly as it intersects with Thursday’s March PCE print.
