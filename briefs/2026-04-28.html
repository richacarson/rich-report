Geopolitics

Iran’s Hormuz-First Proposal Reaches the White House — Trump Signaled Rejection on Monday
Iran delivered a new proposal Monday through Pakistani channels offering to reopen the Strait of Hormuz and end the war in exchange for the lifting of the US naval blockade, with formal nuclear negotiations postponed to a later stage, per Washington Post, Axios, and PBS NewsHour reporting. President Trump and his national security team reviewed the framework with Secretary Rubio and senior advisors, after which the planned Kushner-Witkoff trip to Pakistan was cancelled. CNN sources indicated Trump is unlikely to accept the proposal as structured, and Rubio publicly stated that any framework that defers the nuclear question is unacceptable. The structural impasse from prior briefs persists: Iran insists the blockade be lifted before substantive nuclear talks resume; the US insists on a sequenced deal that addresses enrichment alongside the maritime de-escalation. The conflict has now entered its tenth week, and the IEA continues to characterize the disruption as the largest energy supply shock on record.
Oil Firmed 2% as the Diplomatic Track Stalled — Refining Margins Hold the IOWN Energy Thesis
USO rose 1.75% Monday and BNO advanced 1.84% as the Iran proposal failed to clear, with both crude benchmarks firming on the absence of a near-term diplomatic off-ramp per CNBC and Reuters coverage. The energy sleeve mostly held: VLO advanced 1.02% to $238.25, OKE edged up 0.29% ahead of tonight’s print, and CTRA gained 0.42%, while CVX softened 0.23%. Gold compressed 0.78% to a three-week low as risk capital rotated toward duration-sensitive equity, an unusual signal at this stage of the cycle. Morgan Stanley reiterated that refining margins are unlikely to return to pre-conflict levels in the near term, supporting the structural revenue case for VLO. BP’s Q1 reported profit more than doubled to $3.2B per Reuters — concrete evidence that the integrated energy complex is monetizing the dislocation.
