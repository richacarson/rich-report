Section 02

Geopolitics

Trump Rejects the Hormuz-First Framework — UAE Reportedly Exits OPEC+ This Week
The Iranian proposal that reached the White House Monday — reopen the Strait of Hormuz and end the war in exchange for the lifting of the US naval blockade, with nuclear talks deferred — received a public rejection from Secretary Rubio Tuesday, with President Trump expressing dissatisfaction to advisors per CBS News, Al Jazeera, and CNBC reporting. The structural impasse hardens: Iran insists the blockade lift before substantive nuclear talks, and the US insists any framework must address enrichment alongside the maritime de-escalation. CNN reporting from Day 60 of the conflict indicates the UAE will exit OPEC and OPEC+ this week, a development that would meaningfully reorder the supply-coordination architecture if confirmed. Energy flows through Hormuz remain effectively halted, with roughly 20 million barrels per day of crude, fuels, and petrochemicals affected per IEA characterization — the largest energy supply shock on record. The Panama Canal is now seeing a notable spike in vessel traffic per Reuters as global shipping reroutes around the Middle East disruption.

Refining margins remain materially above pre-conflict levels per Morgan Stanley research. Gold compressed 1.86% and silver fell 3.12% as risk capital rotated toward duration-sensitive equity and energy — an unusual cross-current at this stage of the conflict.
