Markets

Hyperscaler Capex Verdict — Three of Four Mag7 Sell Off After Hours, Only GOOGL Cleared the Bar
The S&P 500 finished essentially flat at roughly 7,116 (–0.02%) and the Nasdaq Composite added 0.61% as XLK gained 0.80%, but the headline tape became secondary to the after-close print sequence. Microsoft beat at $4.27 EPS on $82.89B revenue with Azure constant-currency growth guided to 39%-40% and AI now running at a $37B annualized rate, yet shares fell roughly 3% after-hours per Bloomberg as Microsoft signaled FY26 capex of approximately $190B. Meta beat the top and bottom lines and dropped roughly 6%-7% post-close per Benzinga after lifting 2026 capex guidance from $115B-$135B to $125B-$145B, citing higher component pricing. Amazon delivered AWS at $37.59B (+28% year-over-year, the fastest growth in 15 quarters per CNBC) and EPS of $2.78 versus $1.63 expected, but slipped roughly 3% after-hours on $44.2B Q1 capex and a roughly $200B FY26 spending stance per Seeking Alpha. Alphabet was the singular winner: Q1 revenue $109.9B, Cloud +63% to $20B with a $460B backlog per Fortune, and FY26 capex raised to $180B-$190B; shares rallied roughly 7% after-hours per CNBC. The dispersion is itself the signal — the market is now demanding capex translate to demonstrable revenue acceleration, and only one of the four delivered.
NXPI Surges 25.55% on Physical-AI Strength — The Largest Single-Day Gain Since the 2010 IPO Per CNBC
NXPI closed Wednesday at $289.25 (+25.55%) following Tuesday’s after-close beat — CNBC characterized the move as the biggest single-day gain since NXP’s 2010 IPO. EPS came in at $3.05 against $2.98 consensus on revenue of $3.18B, with Q2 guidance of $3.35B-$3.55B at the lower end above the $3.27B Street estimate per company materials and Investing.com coverage. Management commentary highlighted broad-based strength across automotive, Industrial & IoT, mobile and communications, with explicit callouts on software-defined vehicles and physical AI at the edge. Truist Securities raised its price target to $310 from $255 citing the AI and data center exposure, with Morgan Stanley, Needham, TD Cowen and Raymond James all lifting targets per published research. The print is the cleanest validation of IOWN’s “physical world matters” thesis on this earnings cycle and carries direct read-across to ADI (+1.58% Tuesday), TEL (+0.47%) and the broader analog complex.
QCOM Gains 4.00% on the Day, Adds More After Hours on Hyperscaler Datacenter Chip Win — The Most Consequential IOWN Print of the Week
QCOM closed Wednesday at $156.00 (+4.00%) after reporting fiscal Q2 revenue of $10.6B and non-GAAP EPS of $2.65, at the high end of guidance, with QCT revenue at $9.1B and licensing at $1.4B per company commentary and Bloomberg coverage. Automotive revenue exceeded $5B annualized for the first time, with management guiding to a $6B-plus exit run rate by fiscal year-end. The market response extended into the after-hours session on CEO Cristiano Amon’s commentary that QCOM will begin shipping data center chips to a large hyperscaler ahead of schedule and that the China smartphone cycle has likely bottomed; CNBC reported the stock rallied as much as 16% in late trading. The print materially extends the AI-edge optionality runway and adds a hyperscaler datacenter revenue stream that was not in any sell-side base case. Carson’s SPY $645.80 support remains active and untested, well below current trading.
FOMC Holds at 3.50%-3.75% — Four Dissents Mark the Most Divided Vote Since October 1992 in Powell’s Final Press Conference
The Federal Reserve held the target range steady at 3.50%-3.75% in an 8-4 vote per CNBC and Fox Business reporting, with three of the four dissenters explicitly seeking removal of the easing bias from the statement — the most fractured FOMC vote since October 1992. Chair Powell delivered what he framed as his last press conference as chair, with his term expiring May 15, and stated he intends to remain on the Board of Governors until the Federal Reserve investigation reaches transparency and finality. The dissent count and the hawkish tilt of the dissenters together push the path of least resistance on policy toward higher-for-longer, and TLT closed Wednesday at $85.70 (–0.78%) as the long end repriced. The Powell-to-Warsh succession ahead of the May 15 transition now has the structural backdrop of an unusually divided committee, an inheritance an incoming chair will have to either consolidate or cede.

Section 02

Geopolitics

Trump Hardens the Hormuz Blockade — Brent Surges 6.85% as Iranian Storage Reportedly Approaches Capacity
President Trump publicly committed Wednesday to maintaining the US naval blockade against Iran until Tehran agrees to a comprehensive nuclear framework, per CNBC and CBS News reporting from Day 61 of the conflict. Brent crude surged about 6% to settle at $118.03 per barrel and WTI advanced nearly 7% to $106.88 per barrel per CNBC, with the IOWN data drop showing BNO closing up 6.85% to $58.81 and USO up 7.90% to $150.63. Al Jazeera and Kpler analysis indicated Iranian onshore crude storage could reach capacity within 12 to 22 days if the blockade persists, raising the prospect of forced production curtailment that would further tighten an already constrained physical market. Strait of Hormuz transits remain at roughly 5% of normal volumes per IBTimes coverage, and the IEA continues to characterize the disruption as the largest energy supply shock on record. The diplomatic structure has hardened: Iran’s Hormuz-first proposal received a public rejection, the Kushner-Witkoff Pakistan trip was cancelled, and the administration’s posture is now an explicit linkage of the blockade to nuclear concessions.
IOWN Energy Sleeve Captures the Hormuz Tape — VLO +4.59%, CVX +2.05%, CTRA +3.03% as the Sector Leads
Energy was the clear sector leader Wednesday with XLE at +2.29%, and IOWN’s integrated-energy and refining exposure participated cleanly. VLO advanced 4.59% to $251.30 as refining margins continued to widen against the supply shock; CVX added 2.05% to $192.22; CTRA gained 3.03% to $35.68 ahead of today’s print; and CNX edged up 0.38% to $39.32 with its own report due. OKE softened 0.52% in a give-back from Tuesday’s post-earnings move. The Morgan Stanley framing that refining margins are unlikely to return to pre-conflict levels in the near term remains the structural underpinning of the VLO position. Gold compressed 1.07% and silver fell 2.05% as risk capital continued the unusual rotation toward equity and energy rather than precious metals at this stage of a geopolitical shock — a pattern more consistent with stagflation positioning than with classical risk-off behavior. Five IOWN names report today, two of them in this energy sleeve: CTRA (consensus $0.86) and VLO (consensus $3.18).

With four FOMC dissenters now publicly seeking removal of the easing bias, an in-line or hot core PCE print would meaningfully harden the higher-for-longer rate path that TLT (–0.78% Wednesday to $85.70) is already starting to price.
