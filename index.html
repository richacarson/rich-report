Section 01

U.S. Economics News

Cash Pile-Up

Total assets in U.S. money market funds have reached an unprecedented $7.39 trillion, up 14% from last year.

AI Infrastructure Boom

Construction of data centers has exploded 419% since 2020, while traditional office construction declined 36%, reflecting how AI and digital infrastructure are reshaping commercial real estate investment.

Manufacturing Reshoring

Government incentives like the CHIPS Act have spurred 175% growth in manufacturing-related construction since 2020 — nearly half of which is now computer, electronic, or semiconductor facilities supporting AI and reshoring trends.

Profits May Justify Valuations

The S&P 500's P/E ratio climbed from 15× in 2010 to 27× in Q3 2025, but strong corporate profit margins near all-time highs (13.6%) help validate current price levels rather than signal a bubble.

Tech Dominance

Technology companies now represent 35% of the S&P 500's market cap and generate a disproportionate share of profits, making them key drivers of index gains but also heightening exposure to sector concentration risk.

Unclear Housing Trends

Active listings are expanding at a slower pace, and new-home sales jumped 21% month-over-month in August — suggesting some stabilization, though overall housing sentiment remains unclear.

Section 02

Digital Assets News

Gemini's Solana Card

Crypto exchange Gemini, fresh off its public listing valuing the company at over $3 billion, has launched a Solana-themed credit card allowing users to earn up to 4% back in SOL on purchases, with an auto-staking feature that boosts rewards to a 6.77% APY.

Bitcoin DeFi Demand

Institutional investors holding Bitcoin are increasingly turning to native DeFi solutions like Rootstock and Babylon for yield generation through staking and lending — without leaving the Bitcoin network — despite yields often below 2%.

Stablecoin Cost Savings

According to a KPMG report, stablecoins are poised to transform the $150 trillion annual cross-border payments market by slashing transaction costs by up to 99% and reducing settlement times from days to seconds via public blockchains.

Tokenized Stocks Launch

Solana-based exchange Backpack has partnered with tokenization firm Superstate to integrate the Opening Bell platform, enabling non-U.S. users to trade natively tokenized, SEC-registered U.S. public equities onchain.

BlackRock's IBIT Inflows

BlackRock's flagship iShares Bitcoin Trust (IBIT), the largest spot Bitcoin ETF with $54B in assets, continues attracting strong investor interest with 10 straight days of inflows totaling $134M over the last two sessions — even as Bitcoin price corrects.

Section 03

Geopolitics News

Afghanistan / Pakistan

Neighbors agreed to a 48-hour border ceasefire after deadly clashes and airstrikes, pledging talks while each side accuses the other of fueling violence.

China / U.S.

China's Q3 growth slowed to 4.8% y/y as the trade war and weak demand bite, just ahead of expected Trump–Xi talks.

European Union

The EU's top diplomat said a continent-wide anti-drone "wall" will roll out starting 2026 and be fully operational by 2027 to counter rising incursions.

France

The PM backed suspending Macron's pension-age hike until after 2027, saying any pause must be offset by spending cuts, not a bigger deficit.

Germany

Berlin plans to buy additional F-35s from Lockheed Martin as it boosts defense outlays.

Gaza

Aid trucks moved toward Gaza and preparations to open Rafah resumed after more hostage bodies were returned under the fragile truce.

India

New Delhi accelerated trade talks with Washington to try to clinch a deal by November, even as Russian-oil purchases remain a sticking point.

Japan

Sanae Takaichi is poised to become Japan's first female prime minister after securing a coalition partner to win Tuesday's parliamentary vote.

Syria

Syria's acting leader told Putin in the Kremlin he will honor past agreements with Moscow, as both sides discussed the future of Russian bases.

U.S. / China Trade

Beijing sanctioned U.S. units of Hanwha Ocean and hinted at broader industry retaliation, deepening tit-for-tat port fees and spooking markets. Trump said a 100% tariff on Chinese goods "is not sustainable" but blamed Beijing's rare-earth curbs; a Trump–Xi meeting remains planned.

Section 04

The Good News

🚀

SpaceX Milestone: SpaceX's eleventh Starship test flight marked the most successful yet — launching from Texas, flying halfway around the world, and completing every mission objective without major issues.

✈️

Pilotless Skies: Reliable Robotics and other startups are leading the push toward fully autonomous aircraft, beginning with cargo and military applications. The company recently secured a $17M U.S. Air Force contract to test self-flying cargo planes.

🌋

Geothermal Boom: Geothermal energy firms in the U.S. are spending record sums for federal land leases, with average per-acre prices up 282% to $127, driven by rising data-center power demand and Trump administration support.

Section 05

Dividend Portfolio News

ABT · Abbott Laboratories

Q3 EPS of $1.30 met expectations and revenue slightly missed at $11.37B (+6.9% Y/Y). Abbott reaffirmed 2025 guidance with 7.5–8% organic growth and an EPS range of $5.12–$5.18.

QCOM · Qualcomm

Declared a $0.89 quarterly dividend (2.17% yield), maintaining consistent payouts amid steady cash flows.

Section 06

Growth Portfolio News

AMD · Advanced Micro Devices

Oracle Cloud will deploy 50,000 AMD Instinct MI450 AI chips starting mid-2026, creating the first publicly available AI supercluster powered by AMD GPUs — positioning AMD as a credible challenger to Nvidia's long-held GPU dominance.

COIN · Coinbase

Coinbase launched a U.S. Bitcoin Yield Fund for accredited investors, offering returns via BTC private credit lending and basis trading. Partnering with iTrustCapital, the fund allows investors to hold positions in tax-deferred IRA accounts.

HOOD · Robinhood

Robinhood plans to expand into prediction markets through acquisitions or partnerships, moving beyond retail trading toward becoming a full-scale financial services platform.

NVDA · Nvidia

Nvidia partnered with Firmus Technologies in a $2.9B renewable-powered AI data center project in Australia and, alongside TSMC, completed the first U.S.-made Blackwell wafer in Arizona — a milestone for domestic chipmaking under the CHIPS Act.

TSM · Taiwan Semiconductor

Delivered strong Q3 results with $33.1B revenue (+40.8% Y/Y), 59.5% gross margin, and a new NT$5.00 dividend. Advanced chips (7nm and below) made up 74% of revenue, while management guided for another strong quarter.

XYZ · Block

Block partnered with Grubhub to integrate its Square POS and Cash App Pay systems, modernizing restaurant operations and expanding its presence in the food-ordering and payments ecosystem.
