Executive Summary

3
Macro Context
The Q2 rebalance takes place against the most significant geopolitical supply shock since the 1973 Arab Oil Embargo. The Iran War has shut the Strait of Hormuz, taken ~20% of global oil supply offline, destroyed Qatar's LNG facility, and eliminated 30–35% of global ammonia production. Brent crude surged 57% in March to $119.50/bbl. The Fed is boxed in at 3.50–3.75% with recession probability at 30–42%.
Framework Evolution
This quarter introduces a sector rotation framework — informed by academic evidence (Conover et al. 2008, Moskowitz & Grinblatt 1999, DWS CROCI) — to move beyond equal-weight allocation toward conviction-weighted sector tilts. Quarterly rebalancing is the empirically supported sweet spot.
Key Actions
Dividend (25 → 25): Sell MATX (liquidity) and A (sector rotation + tax loss harvest). Buy NTR (fertilizer) and CTRA (energy).

Growth (24 → 25): Sell AEM, FINV, PDD, TOL. Buy FCX (copper), COHR & CRDO (optical interconnect), VST (nuclear power), MRVL (AI infrastructure).

Sector Rotation: Overweight energy, materials, staples, utilities. Underweight tech in dividend book. Growth tech at 40% but only ~12% at total portfolio level (growth = 30% of aggressive accounts).

Macro & Geopolitical Context

3
Sector Rotation
Capital is flowing decisively out of tech/growth and into the tangible economy. Energy (XLE) +39.5% YTD, Utilities (XLU) +8.3%, Materials (XLB) +8.7%. Technology (XLK) −11.3%, digital assets −47% from ATH. Sector charts confirm: XLE and XLU above both 50 and 200-day MAs (strong uptrend), while XLK, XLY, XLF below both (full downtrend).
Energy Crisis
The Strait of Hormuz closure has removed ~20 million barrels/day of oil transit. Brent crude at ~$110/bbl with analysts forecasting $100–$190 range. Qatar declared force majeure on LNG exports (20% of global supply). US gas benefits as export demand surges. Fertilizer prices up 50% with 30% of global urea trade offline.
Research Foundation
Three original research notes inform positioning: (1) NVDA Optical Interconnects — copper to photonic transition in AI data centers; (2) Persian Gulf Fertilizer Crisis — 30–35% of global ammonia offline; (3) Sector Rotation Academic Review — evidence base for conviction-weighted quarterly tilts.
